Things I Would Do in My First Month at Pure

March 2026

Find the friction

I would map the entire journey from install to paywall and to consumables. I would want to see every single screen where we have money talk and analyze where it is possible to reduce friction. And inversely, where the user feels the pull and has an emotional moment but we don't capitalize on that. Then I would do the same for other dating apps and collect mechanics that we may underutilize.

Software should feel more personal

I would look at daily actions of users who churn, find common patterns (seeing fewer than 20 total ads in the feed), and right when they thought they were out, I'd pull them back in. Some ideas how: extend subscriptions of people who certainly look like they'd cancel — or already did. Give them a second chance and notify them when someone creates a new post.

I would analyze the whole notification system and build honest, contextual notifications. For example, I would find high-activity windows in their specific location and send "Your area is 4× more active than usual right now" at 11pm on a Friday. Which goes to number 3.

Solve the small-area problem

The most common critique of Pure online is the absence of users in smaller areas. We might consider a pricing policy similar to Uber where we change price depending on demand in the area the user is from. Decreasing price in some local areas might fill in the feed.

I would want to see something like a heat map of user activity — see at what time they use the app, see the patterns. Users simply might miss each other in small areas. I would frame it as a math problem where we connect two people who otherwise would have missed each other. Sometimes it is just a perfectly timed notification.

Make iteration fast

Pure is likely to have a subscription engine / CRM. Being inspired by an engineer from Anthropic who automated ads, I would want to reduce any friction so we could iterate on paywalls and subscriptions within days or weeks. Easily do A/B/C tests and get results ASAP. Unless we already have the best system in the world for that.

The right paywall at the right time

On the client side, I would want to have the ability to trigger paywalls on specific actions and trigger a specific paywall variant depending on the user's experience in the app.
